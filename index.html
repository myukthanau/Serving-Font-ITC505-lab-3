How to Deliver a Font to a Client

Fonts are more than just letters on a page; they play a crucial role in how your content is perceived. A well-chosen font can enhance readability, convey brand identity, and evoke emotions. In this example, weâ€™ll be using Open Sans, a modern sans-serif typeface designed by Steve Matteson. Open Sans is known for its clean, friendly appearance and versatility, making it an excellent choice for web design.

Why Choose Open Sans?

  • Readability: Open Sans is optimized for legibility across digital interfaces, ensuring that your content is accessible to all users.
  • Versatility: This font works well for both headings and body text, providing a cohesive look to your website.
  • Wide Availability: Open Sans is widely supported and available through Google Fonts, making it easy to integrate into any project.

Process Overview:

  1. Choose a font and download the necessary font files.
  2. Upload the font files to your server or use a service like Google Fonts.
  3. Define the font using the @font-face rule or embed it via a link.
  4. Apply the font to your HTML elements.

This method allows the browser to download the font and render it correctly for users visiting your site.
